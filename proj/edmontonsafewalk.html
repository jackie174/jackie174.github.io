Edmonton Safewalk This website is created by our team. This website is non-profit, it helps people to walk home. Edmonton Safe Walk is a pilot project created in partnership between Sisters Dialogue, the Edmonton Federation of Community Leagues (EFCL), and the City of Edmonton. Community Services-Learning (University of Alberta) fostered a partnership for the development of this web app with students in the Department of Computer Sciences. Edmonton SafeWalk hopes to create more safety and ease for underrepresented groups through building relationships and community connections- while walking! Due to ongoing Islamophobic incidences in Edmonton, many women are not feeling safe walking to and from transit, jobs, or even around their neighbourhoods for exercise. For this pilot, we are focusing on supporting Black, racialized, and visibly Muslim women, though all are welcome to access this service. For the summer 2022 pilot, walks are limited to communities in Northeast Edmonton. We hope to expand the program in the future and would love to hear from you. SafeWalk Volunteers will receive training in anti-oppression and allyship in a community context. This website provides an app where Edmonton citizens can request a walk and be paired with a volunteer to walk together at a specific time. You can book a walk up to 24 hours before the time you want to meet. The app will mainly be used as a web app that can be accessed through any computer or mobile device.
