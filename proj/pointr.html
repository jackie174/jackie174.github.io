About 3D Model Inpainting

Peng Cheng, Shiqi Zhang

3D shape completion is a technique to fill in missing or incomplete parts of a 3D shape or model. It is useful in various fields, including computer graphics, computer vision, and robotics. 3D shape completion can be used to improve the accuracy and realism of 3D models, which are often used in virtual and augmented reality applications, as well as in simulations and other types of interactive experiences. This paper proposes potential improvements in the 3D shape completion model by adding a network called LBCNN. LBCNN can be used to improve the performance of 3D shape completion in several ways. For example, LBCNN can be trained to recognize and classify 3D shapes based on their appearance, allowing them to identify and fill in missing parts of a shape accurately. In addition, LBCNN can be used to analyze and understand the underlying structure and geometry of a 3D shape, which can be useful for predicting how missing parts of the shape should be completed. Furthermore, we compare a few networks without LBCNN using KITTI and PCN datasets.

The result is not significantly increased, but it has improved the performance of 3D inpainting. We mainly test the KITTI data set and get a satisfactory result. More data sets and time must be tested to provide a more thorough analysis of our method. We need to find a way to reduce the data set, and we especially need to test the PCN data set. We also need to explore the Transformer to improve its performance.
